About the data

Methodology

How this screen is built, what it includes, and the limitations you should keep in mind.

The screen

Every quarter we read all Form 13F filings from SEC EDGAR and keep only institutions that are large and concentrated, to isolate high-conviction, value-oriented managers. A filing qualifies when it has:

  • More than ${{ criteria.min_aum_b }} billion in disclosed 13F assets, and
  • at least {{ criteria.min_holdings }} distinct companies (so single- and double-stock vehicles — operating companies reporting one strategic stake — are excluded), and
  • less than {{ criteria.max_etf_pct }}% of assets in ETFs or index funds (a mostly-ETF book is a passive basket, not a stock-picker), and
  • is concentrated by either measure:
    • {{ criteria.max_holdings }} or fewer distinct companies held, or
    • its top {{ criteria.top_n }} positions make up at least {{ criteria.top_n_min_pct }}% of disclosed AUM, while still holding {{ criteria.max_holdings_weighted }} or fewer distinct companies.
A large book paired with either a short holdings list or a heavy top-10 weighting is the signature of a manager who concentrates capital in their best ideas, rather than indexing broadly. The second test catches managers who carry a modest tail of small positions but still run a genuinely concentrated portfolio — while the {{ criteria.max_holdings_weighted }}-company cap on that test keeps out broad mutual-fund complexes that merely happen to be top-heavy.

Manager deep-dives keep full history

Whether a manager qualifies can change from quarter to quarter — a book can drift just over or under the holdings line. Once a manager qualifies in any of the recent quarters we track, we keep its filings for all of those quarters so each deep-dive shows a continuous timeline and accurate quarter-over-quarter moves. Quarters where the manager didn't meet the criteria are clearly marked (with a ○) and are excluded from that quarter's screened universe and rankings.

Filer types

13F filings come from many kinds of institutions, not just stock-pickers. We label each filer by type and exclude the kinds that aren't fundamental, concentrated fund managers: market-makers / trading desks, operating and holding companies (corporations reporting strategic stakes), sovereign wealth funds, central banks and pensions, and banks / insurers. Private-equity / venture firms and foundations / endowments are kept, since some run genuinely concentrated equity books. Types come from a transparent name heuristic with per-filer overrides for the cases a name can't settle (and to protect real managers whose name looks like an operating company). The heuristic is approximate; corrections are welcome.

How figures are computed

  • AUM is the sum of a filer's disclosed long positions. Filings that report values in thousands (before 2023) are normalized to whole dollars.
  • Holdings are aggregated by company (the first six CUSIP digits), so multiple share classes or shared-discretion rows collapse into one position.
  • Quarter-over-quarter moves compare share counts against the manager's previous screened quarter, classifying each position as new, added, trimmed, or exited.
  • Amended filings (13F-HR/A) supersede the original for that period.

Update cadence

13F filings are due 45 days after each quarter-end. Shortly after each deadline (mid-February, May, August, and November) the site automatically ingests the new quarter and rebuilds.

Limitations

  • 13F discloses long U.S.-listed positions only — no short positions, cash, non-U.S. listings, or most derivatives.
  • Data is reported with up to a 45-day lag, so it is not real-time.
  • Managers may obtain confidential treatment to omit holdings; such filings are excluded from the screen rather than counted as concentrated.
  • Figures are parsed from public filings and may contain errors.
